this is a rant about Apple, a company that i absolutely despise, and there are a multitude of reasons why.

starting with the basics: the price. you should not be paying $1000+ for an iPhone or an iPad. and that price should not keep going up with every new iteration when there are barely any improvements to the actual device. genuinely the things they brag about in these iPhone announcements are things like the notch being slightly smaller... or the camera being slightly different... or things on the home screen looking slightly different or being in slightly different places (and sometimes also things like durability, but literally everyone has a screen protector and a case so idk what the point is). sorry dude, not enough to justify your prices. we already can't afford to live.

then there's the fact that if i ask you how many years you've had your iPhone / when you're planning on replacing it, your answer will probably be 2 or maybe 3 years. that's not a long time, and again, these things are a ripoff, so that just sucks. but why are you replacing it so quickly? i'll give you a hint: it's probably not because of the notch or the camera. it's probably because in a few short years, you'll be forced to watch as your iPhone stops getting updates, stops supporting any of the features or apps you like, and slowly becomes an obsolete piece of junk. Apple doesn't need to do that. they have trillions of dollars; they can afford to keep supporting your version of iOS. the only reason why your iPhone becomes useless so quickly is because Apple wants you to buy the next (painfully mediocre) one sooner and give them even more money. you basically pay a subscription fee to keep having an iPhone (this actually has a name; it's called "planned obsolescence" and you can look it up if you're curious).

then there's Apple doing (in my opinion) one of the scummiest things i've ever seen from a company. you Apple users always talk about connectivity, and how your iPhone seamlessly connects to your MacBook and your iPad and your AirPods and whatever, and i'll give credit where it's due: they are good at doing that. HOWEVER, they do that because they want you to stay inside the Apple ecosystem and keep buying only Apple stuff forever. if you're ever planning on connecting with any device that doesn't have Apple's logo on it, prepare for a nightmare (this also has a name: "vendor lock-in"). if you've ever texted me (an Android user) with an iPhone, you've probably noticed that everything is just absolutely terrible, including image quality, group chats, and things like stickers and reactions. contrary to popular belief, that's not because my phone is a piece of garbage; it's because Apple is a living, breathing mountain of garbage. you can read more about this here and elsewhere, but i will attempt to summarize: for a while Apple handled communication with Android devices using two things called SMS and MMS, which is the awful stuff you're used to seeing. SMS and MMS are OLD. for YEARS there has been better technology, RCS, which solves those problems. Android already adopted RCS for all of its devices, but Apple wouldn't do it... for years... until iOS 18. nothing about that seems like an accident to me. here's what i think: Apple wanted you to text your friends who have Android phones, have a really crappy experience, and get extremely frustrated and urge your friends to buy iPhones. you were basically being used as a marketing tool, and peer pressure was their way of acquiring a new customer. surely i'm not the only one who thinks that's disgusting and wrong.

but wait i'm not done. i also hate HEIC, the image format for iPhone camera photos that has no reason to exist and (again) basically doesn't work with anything non-Apple, like genuinely how do you open these files without converting them to JPG i literally can't figure it out. there's also the Lightning port, another stupid attempt to force you to buy exclusive Apple stuff, which they only changed to USB-C because regulators forced them to (NOT to improve your experience). and then there's the whole "right to repair" thing, and this is where i become a little less precise because i don't know as much about this topic, but from what i understand, it is unreasonably hard to fix broken parts in your Apple devices, and if something breaks, you're basically forced to bring it to the Apple store and get them to repair it, which is way more expensive than repairing it on your own. so again, making your experience worse so they can profit more.

so hopefully these 5000+ characters have convinced you to abandon your cult-like devotion to this brand that does not care about you at all. and i certainly hope that when you get annoyed because of how terrible texting is, you understand that we Android users are around 50x as annoyed as you for the exact same reason, and instead of fighting one another, we should all be on the same team.
